Welcome to the Imperial Circles of the Holy Roman Empire in the seventeenth (?) century! This interactive journey invites you to explore how a complex historical structure governed itself across multiple boundaries of jurisdiction, confession and language. The Imperial Circles (Reichskreise) were not abstract administrative units that merely existed on paper: they actively coordinated key functions of the Empire such as military defence, regulated currencies, mobility of goods and people, and social issues (poverty) at a time when no overarching welfare state existed. Their work shaped everyday life from the coins people handled to the soldiers they quartered. Our map shows the outline of the Imperial Circles in XXXX and will offer geographical orientation during your exploration of the key functions performed by the Circles. To start, select one key theme and travel back in time to learn about real historical problems drawn from contemporary sources. Each controversy will require you to take decisions and negotiate solutions. Can the Empire enjoy peace and security under your guidance?
