War and Imperial Defence

Imperial defence was one of the most demanding and controversial tasks of the early modern Imperial Circles. While the Holy Roman Empire lacked a standing army, the Circles coordinated troop levies, financing, provisioning, and logistics in times of war. This system relied on negotiated quotas, legal precedents, and constant debate over obligation and capacity. Conflicts such as the Ottoman wars, the War of the Spanish Succession, or post-war armament plans exposed tensions between imperial authority, territorial sovereignty, and local resources. You will navigate disputes over troop numbers, funding formulas, and the meaning of collective security, voiced by princes, powerful cities, and jurists. Can you help the Imperial Circles successfully perform their role as mediators between empire-wide demands and regional realities?
